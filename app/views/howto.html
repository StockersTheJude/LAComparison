How to use this tool

You can view and compare the performance of local authorities in four areas of service delivery:

  • Children and vulnerable people
  • Health, social care and integration
  • Housing, planning and transport
  • Safer, cleaner, stronger communities

The tool will only show data for the services provided by the local authority you selected.

For example, selecting a district council will not produce results for transport. This is because the county council in that area manages transport. So you would need to select the county council.

To view performance data for one local authority

  1. Choose a local authority in the first drop down menu
  2. Click Apply Filters

To compare your chosen local authority with up to three other local authorities

  1. Choose a local authority in the first drop down menu
  2. Choose up to three more local authorities in the next drop down menus
  3. Click Apply Filters
  

Your selected local authorities will show on the graph as coloured dots.  The grey dots on the graph represent other local authorities in England.

This means that you can see how the ones you selected compare to them.

The median line shows the average performance.  Either side of the median line is positive or negative (performance) or high or low (expenditure).

Underneath the graphs is a table of data sources for the selected local authorities. You can use this to view more detailed information.

Detailed explanation of the methodology

To plot local authority data we use the median absolute deviation (MAD). We preferred the median to the mean because outliers can skew the mean value in each dataset. An example would be temporary accommodation where there are some significant negative outliers.

On the graph, you can see how local authorities compare to the median local authority value. Their distance from the median line shows the comparison.

To find this, we take the difference between the value of the chosen authority and the median local authority, and divide it by MAD.

A local authority's score is 'positive' or 'negative when it is more than one MAD above or below the median (MADs are shown along the bottom of the graph). These scores are in a summary table for the first selected authority.

Local authorities more than two MADS above or below the median imply exceptional performance. This will often be due to unique local area characteristics such as low populations.

Public consensus is used to find positive and negative directions. For example, fewer roads in need of repair is a positive. In this example, negative performance means a higher percentage of roads needing repair.

Any authority that scores 7.5 MADs above or below the median will be grouped at the end of the scale. You should consider this when viewing these local authorities and their actual values.
