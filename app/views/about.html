About this tool

This interactive tool enables you to see and compare local authority metrics for the services they provide. 

The first version of the tool displays 16 local authority outcome metrics, based on 2020/2021 financial year upper (county council) and lower tier (district council) local authority structures. 

Limitations of the data set

The tool does not include metrics on:

  • police
  • fire
  • waste
  • national parks
  • combined authorities
  • 2020-2021 merged authorities of North and West Northamptonshire are excluded

This tool will only display data for your chosen service if you have selected the correct tier of local authority under which that service sits. You will need to know which tier of local authority within your chosen area/county provides the service you want to view data on. You can check this information here (Source: Office for National Statistics).

Types of local authority

Shire districts have responsibility for:

  • housing
  • leisure and recreation
  • environmental health
  • waste collection
  • planning applications
  • local taxation collection

Shire counties have responsibility for:

  • education
  • highways
  • transport planning
  • planning applications
  • local taxation collection

Unitaries have responsibility for both shire district and shire county level services.   This means they are responsible for:

  • education
  • housing
  • leisure and recreation
  • libraries
  • highways
  • transport planning
  • environmental health
  • social care
  • waste collection and disposal
  • strategic planning
  • planning applications
  • local taxation collection

The Greater London Authority (GLA) runs Transport for London.  This means that London Boroughs are not responsible for highways or transport planning.

In London, Greater Manchester (excluding Wigan and Merseyside), joint waste management authorities are responsible for waste collection. In Manchester, it is the Greater Manchester Combined Authority. In London, there are four joint waste authorities. These are:

  • West Londonn
  • North London
  • Western Riverside
  • East London

Data for Buckinghamshire may be missing where the data has not been reported yet for the new unitary authority.

The Common Council of the City of London and the Council of the Isles of Scilly are often called 'sui generis' or unique authorities.  This means that they function like unitary authorities, even though they cater for very small populations.  Because of their uniqueness, they are not included in the local authority comparisons.

For details of the latest Ofsted rankings of schools, please refer to the Compare School Performance Service.  This data is updated and maintained separately by the Department for Education.

We will update this interactive tool on a quarterly basis.  If new data becomes available between updates, it will not be shown until the next update.
